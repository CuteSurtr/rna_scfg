# Literature references

The PDFs of the foundational papers for this project were kept locally during development but are not redistributed in this repository. The citations below identify every source used in `docs/lit_review.md` and throughout the library documentation.

1. Nussinov, R. and Jacobson, A. B. (1980). Fast algorithm for predicting the secondary structure of single stranded RNA. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 77(11), 6309 to 6313. DOI: 10.1073/pnas.77.11.6309

2. Zuker, M. and Stiegler, P. (1981). Optimal computer folding of large RNA sequences using thermodynamics and auxiliary information. *Nucleic Acids Research*, 9(1), 133 to 148. DOI: 10.1093/nar/9.1.133

3. McCaskill, J. S. (1990). The equilibrium partition function and base pair binding probabilities for RNA secondary structure. *Biopolymers*, 29(6 to 7), 1105 to 1119. DOI: 10.1002/bip.360290621

4. Sakakibara, Y., Brown, M., Hughey, R., Mian, I. S., Sjolander, K., Underwood, R. C., and Haussler, D. (1994). Stochastic context free grammars for tRNA modeling. *Nucleic Acids Research*, 22(23), 5112 to 5120. DOI: 10.1093/nar/22.23.5112

5. Eddy, S. R. and Durbin, R. (1994). RNA sequence analysis using covariance models. *Nucleic Acids Research*, 22(11), 2079 to 2088. DOI: 10.1093/nar/22.11.2079

6. Knudsen, B. and Hein, J. (1999). RNA secondary structure prediction using stochastic context free grammars and evolutionary history. *Bioinformatics*, 15(6), 446 to 454. DOI: 10.1093/bioinformatics/15.6.446

7. Knudsen, B. and Hein, J. (2003). Pfold: RNA secondary structure prediction using stochastic context free grammars. *Nucleic Acids Research*, 31(13), 3423 to 3428. DOI: 10.1093/nar/gkg614

8. Dowell, R. D. and Eddy, S. R. (2004). Evaluation of several lightweight stochastic context free grammars for RNA secondary structure prediction. *BMC Bioinformatics*, 5, 71. DOI: 10.1186/1471 2105 5 71

9. Durbin, R., Eddy, S. R., Krogh, A., and Mitchison, G. (1998). *Biological Sequence Analysis: Probabilistic Models of Proteins and Nucleic Acids*. Cambridge University Press. ISBN 978 0 521 62971 3

10. Turner, D. H. and Mathews, D. H. (2010). NNDB: the nearest neighbor parameter database for predicting stability of nucleic acid secondary structure. *Nucleic Acids Research*, 38 (Database), D280 to D282. DOI: 10.1093/nar/gkp892
